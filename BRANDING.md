# FluxParser - Brand Story

## The Name

**FluxParser** is named after Isaac Newton's "fluxions" - the original term he coined in 1671 for what we now call derivatives.

## Historical Context

### Newton's Fluxions (1671)

In his groundbreaking work *"Method of Fluxions"* (written 1671, published 1736), Isaac Newton introduced:

- **Fluent quantities** (variables that change over time)
- **Fluxions** (rates of change - what we now call derivatives)

Newton wrote about "flowing quantities" and their "fluxions" - the instantaneous rate at which they flow or change. This was his notation for calculus, developed independently from Leibniz's differential notation.

### Why This Matters

FluxParser embodies this historical connection:

1. **Symbolic Differentiation** - Our core feature traces back to Newton's fluxions
2. **Newton-Raphson Method** - Named after Isaac Newton (and Joseph Raphson who simplified it in 1690)
3. **Numerical Methods** - Building on 350+ years of mathematical innovation
